bug-summary: |
  pdfjs-dist 5.x getDocument({ data }) transfers the underlying ArrayBuffer to its
  worker via postMessage with a transfer list, leaving the source Uint8Array
  detached (byteLength === 0). Commit 1dca433 added an inline defensive copy INSIDE
  loadPdf's getDocument call. But replacePlanPdf had its OWN getDocument call as a
  pre-validation probe (line 286) — an unprotected second caller. That probe
  detached newBytes before the downstream applyReplacePlanPdf -> loadPdfFromBytes
  -> loadPdf path could re-read it; loadPdf's new Uint8Array(u8) line then threw
  "Cannot perform Construct on a detached ArrayBuffer". The dimension-mismatch
  path's `pendingBytes: newBytes` return (line 310) was a latent symptom of the
  same root cause — also a now-detached buffer.

helper-introduction: |
  cloneForPdfWorker(bytes) in src/renderer/src/lib/pdf-setup.ts is now the SINGLE
  source of truth for the pdfjs ArrayBuffer-transfer defensive copy. Every site in
  the renderer that hands a Uint8Array to pdfjsLib.getDocument({ data }) MUST
  call it. Two production sites today: useProject.replacePlanPdf (probe + pendingBytes)
  and usePdfDocument.loadPdf (the original 1dca433 site, refactored to share the
  helper). JSDoc comment in pdf-setup.ts documents the hazard so any future caller
  understands why the wrap is mandatory.
