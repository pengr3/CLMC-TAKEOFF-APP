12. Verify the number inside the dark pin is rendered in WHITE (auto-contrast — D-23). The previous light pin still has its number in BLACK.

### Section C — Linear / Area / Perimeter measurement legibility (B2/B3 + D-31/D-34)

13. Click the Linear tool. Draw a polyline with 5+ segments. Double-click to end.
14. In the popup, type "Wall A", pick any color, click Save Markup.
15. The committed polyline shows a label on canvas — JUST the measurement, e.g. `12.4 m`. NO name text. Label sits at the GEOMETRIC center of the line (B2 — not at an arbitrary vertex; for a straight-ish line it reads as the visual middle).
16. Click the Area tool. Trace a closed polygon (click first-point to close). Name it "Tiling Area 1". Save.
17. The polygon is filled at ~20% opacity and its stroke is the chosen color. Inside the polygon: a single-line label shows just the area, e.g. `38.2 m²`. NO name text.
18. Click the Perimeter tool. Trace a closed polygon. Name it "Room 2". Save.
19. The polygon shows a single-line label: `P: 24.6 m  A: 38.2 m²`. NO name text.
20. Zoom in to maximum — labels grow and stay sharp.
21. Zoom out to fit — labels shrink (expected at fit zoom they read small, per D-34 trade-off). No "snap" on any zoom transition (B4 regression).

### Section D — Hover + name discovery (D-30)

22. Hover a Linear markup — tooltip shows "Wall A — 12.4 m". Hover an Area — tooltip shows "Tiling Area 1 — 38.2 m²". Hover a Perimeter — tooltip shows "Room 2 — P:24.6 m A:38.2 m²". Hover a Count pin — tooltip shows "Light Switch — #N".

### Section E — Color model + recolor (D-25/D-26/D-27/D-28/D-29)
